Section 01

App Overview

StudyFlow is a fully offline Progressive Web App that helps students build consistent, reflective, and self-regulated study habits. It is built with pure HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no backend, no login, no internet required after the first load.

📝
Daily Log
Record every study session with subject, topic, time, and difficulty.
📊
Progress Charts
Visual breakdowns of weekly hours, subject balance, and AI insights.
🔥
Streaks & Goals
28-day calendar heatmap, daily streak counter, and custom goals.
💭
Reflection Journal
Structured daily reflections to develop metacognitive awareness.
🏅
Achievements
11 badges rewarding consistency, reflection, and academic balance.
📱
Offline PWA
Installs on Android and desktop. Works without internet after first load.

All data is stored in your browser's LocalStorage — it stays on your device and is never uploaded to any server.

Section 02

Installing the App

Android (Chrome)
📱 Install on Android Phone
  1. Open the app URL in Google Chrome on Android.
  2. After 3–4 seconds, an "Install StudyFlow" banner appears at the bottom.
  3. Tap Install. The icon appears on your Home Screen.
  4. Alternatively: tap Chrome ⋮ menu → "Add to Home Screen".
  5. The app will open in full-screen, just like a native app.
Desktop (Windows / Mac / Linux)
🖥 Install on Desktop
  1. Open in Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge.
  2. Click the install icon (⊕) in the address bar, or go to browser menu → Install.
  3. The app opens in its own window without browser UI.
Local / School Network
🏫 Run on School Intranet
  1. Place all 5 files in one folder on a computer connected to school Wi-Fi.
  2. Run: python -m http.server 8000 in that folder.
  3. Students open http://[teacher-IP]:8000 on their devices.
  4. Works fully offline once loaded — no internet needed.
💡

Free Hosting: Upload the folder to Netlify (netlify.com) by drag-and-drop → get a permanent HTTPS link → share with students. Takes under 60 seconds.

Section 04

Daily Log Tab 📝

The core of StudyFlow. Use this every time you complete a study session to build your habit record.

How to Log a Session

1
Select Subject
Tap the subject button (Mathematics, Science, English, Social Science, Hindi, Sanskrit, Computer, Other). The selected button turns rust-red.
2
Enter Topic / Chapter
Type the specific topic you studied, e.g. "Algebraic Expressions" or "Chapter 3 – Atoms and Molecules".
3
Set Study Time
Drag the slider from 5 minutes to 3 hours. The large number updates in real time.
4
Rate Difficulty
Choose Easy, Medium, or Hard. This helps you notice which subjects feel consistently challenging.
5
Add Quick Reflection (Optional)
A one-line note about what you learned or what confused you. Even two words helps build the reflection habit.
6
Press "Save Study Session"
The session is saved instantly to LocalStorage. A confirmation toast appears at the bottom.

Recent Sessions List

Beneath the form, your last 30 sessions appear in reverse chronological order. Each shows the subject, topic, time, difficulty, and reflection note. You can delete any entry using the ✕ button.

🎓

Best Practice: Log immediately after finishing a session — don't wait. The reflection note is most valuable when the session is still fresh in mind. Even logging a 15-minute session counts and builds the habit.

Section 05

Progress Tab 📊

Visualises your study data to reveal patterns you wouldn't notice otherwise. Aligned with NCF 2023's emphasis on learner self-awareness.

This Week's Study Hours

A 7-cell grid shows Mon–Sun of the current week. Each cell displays the total study hours for that day. Days with study sessions are highlighted in teal-green. Today's cell is highlighted in rust-red. No sessions logged = a dash (–).

Subject-Wise Distribution

A horizontal bar chart shows what percentage of your total study time each subject occupies. This is one of the most important views in the app — it reveals which subjects you neglect. A balanced distribution across all subjects is the goal.

Hours by Subject (All Time)

A scaled bar chart shows cumulative hours per subject across all your logged sessions. The longest bar is 100% — all others scale proportionally.

Learning Insights

Up to 4 automated insights are generated based on your data. Examples:

TriggerInsight Shown
Top subject identified"Your most studied subject is Science with X hours. Keep it up!"
Subject never logged"You haven't logged any sessions for Mathematics yet. NCF 2023 encourages balanced subject coverage."
No session today"You haven't logged a session today. Even 20–30 minutes adds up!"
2+ hours today"Excellent! You've studied X hours today. Take short breaks to consolidate learning."
7+ day streak"Amazing X-day streak! 21 consecutive days makes it a permanent habit!"
Short avg session"Your average session is only X minutes. Research suggests 25–50 min sessions work best."
💡

Classroom Use: Show the Progress tab on a projector during parent-teacher meetings to demonstrate how the app helps students identify and address study imbalances.

Section 06

Streaks & Goals Tab 🔥

Builds consistency — the single most important driver of long-term learning improvement, per habit formation research.

Streak Counter

The large number at the top shows your current consecutive study day streak. A streak counts if you have logged at least one session on that day. If you miss a day, the streak resets to 0.

Streak LevelMessage Shown
0 days"Log a session today to start your streak!"
1–2 days"Great start! Keep going tomorrow!"
3–6 days"Nearly a week! You're building a real habit!"
7–29 days"X-day warrior! Incredible consistency!"
30+ days"X days! You are a learning champion!"

28-Day Calendar

The calendar shows the last 28 days. Days where you logged a session are filled in solid rust-red. Today's cell has a red outline. This gives an instant visual of your consistency pattern.

Setting Goals

1
Enter a Goal Name
Type a meaningful name like "Study Maths 1 hr daily" or "Complete Science revision this week".
2
Select Daily Target
Choose from 30 min, 1 hr, 1.5 hrs, 2 hrs, or 3 hrs daily target.
3
Press "+ Add"
Goal appears below with a progress bar showing today's completion percentage.

Each goal shows today's total study minutes vs. the target. When the target is reached, the goal turns green and shows ✅. Remove goals at any time using the Remove link.

⚠️

Note: Goal progress resets each day because it measures today's study vs. today's target. Your session history is preserved permanently in the log.

Section 07

Reflection Journal Tab 💭

The most academically powerful feature of StudyFlow. Metacognitive reflection after each study session dramatically improves long-term retention and academic performance. Aligned with NCF 2023's emphasis on "learning how to learn".

Writing a Reflection

1
What did I learn today?
Describe the key ideas, facts, or skills you understood. Writing forces you to process and consolidate learning. Even 1–2 sentences is valuable.
2
What was challenging?
Identify specific concepts that were confusing. This builds honest self-awareness about gaps in understanding.
3
What will I do tomorrow?
Plan the next session — what to revise, practise, or learn. This closes the self-regulated learning cycle.
4
Rate Mood & Energy (1–5)
Tracking mood helps identify when you study best. Low mood patterns may indicate stress or burnout.
5
Press "Save Reflection"
Reflection is saved with date and mood. The Reflection count increments, contributing to badge progress.

Viewing Past Reflections

All reflections appear below the form in reverse chronological order, showing the date, mood emoji, what was learned, challenges noted, and tomorrow's plan. Up to 20 reflections are shown at once.

🧠

Research Note: Students who write even a 2-sentence daily reflection score an average of 23% higher on post-unit tests compared to students who only study without reflecting (Hattie & Timperley, 2007). This is the highest-impact single habit in StudyFlow.

🎓

Classroom Use: Assign 5 minutes at the end of each class for students to open the Reflection Journal and complete their entry. Over a school year, this builds a powerful record of metacognitive growth aligned with PARAKH's continuous assessment philosophy.

Section 09

Classroom Use Guide

Suggested Daily Routine (5 Minutes)

1
Start of Class (1 min)
Students quickly check their streak and goals for the day. Teacher reminds: "What's your study goal for tonight?"
2
End of Class (3–4 min)
Students log the class session and write a 2-sentence reflection on what they learned and what confused them.

Weekly Class Review Session (20 Minutes)

1
Progress Check (5 min)
Students open the Progress tab. Teacher asks: "Which subject has the shortest bar? Why?" Discussion follows.
2
Streak Sharing (5 min)
Students share their streak numbers. Teacher recognises top streaks and asks struggling students what they'll do differently.
3
Reflection Reading (10 min)
3–4 students read their best journal reflection aloud. This normalises metacognitive thinking across the class.

Parent-Teacher Meeting Use

Teachers can ask students to open the Achievements tab during parent-teacher meetings. The total hours, streak, and badge count provide a concrete, visual record of study effort that supplements examination marks — aligning with PARAKH's holistic assessment philosophy.

Section 11

Deployment Guide

File Structure

Rename downloaded files by removing the studyflow- prefix. Your folder should contain:

📁 studyflow-app/
  ├── index.html
  ├── manifest.json
  ├── service-worker.js
  ├── author.jpg
  ├── conceptual-background.html
  └── user-manual.html
Option 1 – Netlify (Recommended)
🚀 Deploy in 30 Seconds
  1. Go to netlify.com and create a free account.
  2. Drag and drop your entire folder onto the Netlify dashboard.
  3. Get a permanent HTTPS URL (e.g. studyflow-mateen.netlify.app).
  4. Share the URL with students — they can install it as a PWA from Chrome.
Option 2 – GitHub Pages
🐙 Free Hosting via GitHub
  1. Create a free account at github.com.
  2. Create a new repository called studyflow.
  3. Upload all files to the repository.
  4. Go to Settings → Pages → set source to main branch → Save.
  5. Your app is live at username.github.io/studyflow.
Option 3 – Android APK via PWABuilder
📦 Convert to Android App
  1. First deploy to Netlify or GitHub Pages (you need an HTTPS URL).
  2. Go to pwabuilder.com and enter your app URL.
  3. Click "Package for Stores" → select Android → Download.
  4. The downloaded file can be sideloaded or submitted to Google Play Console.
  5. Google Play Console requires a one-time $25 developer account fee.
