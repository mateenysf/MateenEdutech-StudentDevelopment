Getting Started & Installation

First Launch

  1. Open the index.html file in your browser (Chrome or Edge recommended for full PWA support)
  2. The Landing Page will appear showing the app introduction and author information
  3. Click the "Launch Study Planner" button to enter the app
  4. The app will load with sample subjects (Mathematics, Science, English, Social Science, Hindi) pre-configured
  5. Your data is automatically saved to your browser's local storage — no account or internet needed
✅ Good news: All your data (timetable, study hours, exams, notes) is saved automatically on your device. You never need to manually save anything!

Installing as a Mobile App (PWA)

📱

On Android (Chrome)

Tap the 3-dot menu → "Add to Home Screen" → Confirm. App installs like a native Android app.

💻

On Desktop (Chrome/Edge)

Click the install icon (⊕) in the browser address bar, or go to Settings → "Install StudyPlanner"

📴

Offline Use

Once installed, the app works fully offline. No internet required for any feature.

Section 2

Managing Subjects

The Subjects tab is your foundation. All other features (timetable, tracker, exams, notes) are organized around your subjects. Set this up first.

Adding a New Subject

  1. Go to the 📚 Subjects tab in the navigation bar
  2. Enter the Subject Name (e.g., "Chemistry", "Computer Science")
  3. Choose a Color by clicking the color picker — this color will appear throughout the app to identify this subject
  4. Set a Weekly Target in hours (e.g., 3 means you aim to study this subject for 3 hours per week)
  5. Select Difficulty Level: Easy, Medium, or Hard/Weak Subject
  6. Click "Add Subject" — the subject appears in your list immediately
💡 NEP 2020 Tip: Mark your weakest subject as "Hard" — the AI Timetable Generator will allocate more study slots to it automatically, reflecting NCF 2023's principle of addressing learning gaps systematically.

Understanding Subject Progress Bars

Each subject shows a colored progress bar indicating how much of your weekly target you've completed. A full bar means you've hit your target for that subject this week.

Colors: 🟢 Green = on track | 🟡 Yellow = partial progress | Empty = not started

Deleting a Subject

  1. Find the subject in your list
  2. Click the red 🗑️ trash icon on the right
  3. The subject is removed (note: past study sessions for this subject are preserved in history)

Section 3

Building Your Weekly Timetable

The Timetable tab allows you to create a visual 7-day study schedule. This is the heart of the app — NCF 2023 emphasizes that organized learning requires a structured plan.

Method 1: Manual Slot Assignment

  1. Go to the 📅 Timetable tab
  2. You will see a grid with days (Mon–Sun) across the top and time slots (4:00 PM to 8:30 PM) down the side
  3. Click any empty "+" slot in the grid
  4. A popup will appear showing the day and time of that slot
  5. Select a subject from the dropdown
  6. Click "✅ Assign" — the slot fills with your subject's color and name
  7. Repeat for all the slots you want to fill

Method 2: AI Auto-Generator (Recommended)

  1. Scroll down to the "Quick Timetable Generator" section
  2. Choose how many Study Hours per Day you have available (1–4 hours)
  3. Set your preferred Start Time for daily study
  4. Click "🤖 Generate AI Timetable"
  5. The app automatically fills all 7 days with a balanced schedule, giving more slots to subjects marked "Hard"
🤖 How the AI works: The generator sorts your subjects by difficulty (Hard → Medium → Easy) and distributes them across all days. Hard subjects get more slots, ensuring that weak areas receive the extra practice they need — aligned with PARAKH's competency-building philosophy.

Clearing the Timetable

Click the red "🗑️ Clear All" button to reset the entire timetable and start fresh.

Reading the Color Legend

Below the timetable grid, a legend shows each subject with its assigned color. Use this as a quick visual reference to ensure your schedule has a balanced mix of colors.

Section 4

Study Time Tracker

The Tracker tab contains a live study timer that records your actual study sessions. This creates an honest log of how you spend your study time — a metacognitive tool advocated by NCF 2023.

Starting a Study Session

  1. Go to the ⏱️ Tracker tab
  2. Select the subject you are about to study from the dropdown
  3. Click "▶ Start Session" — the timer begins counting up
  4. Study with full focus while the timer runs
  5. When done, click "⏹ Stop" — the session is automatically saved

Pausing a Session

  1. Click "⏸ Pause" to temporarily stop the timer (e.g., for a short break)
  2. The button changes to "▶ Resume"
  3. Click Resume to continue the session — paused time is not counted in your study hours
✅ Best Practice: Always select the correct subject before starting. Your tracker data is the basis for the Dashboard analytics, Weekly Summary, and Badge system. Accurate tracking = accurate insights.

Reading Weekly Hours Report

Below the timer, a bar chart shows hours studied per subject this week vs. your target. Green bars indicate you're meeting your target; shorter bars reveal which subjects need more attention.

Session History

The last 10 sessions are displayed with subject name, date/time, and duration. Use this to review your recent study patterns and ensure you're maintaining balance across subjects.

Section 5

Exam Countdown Manager

The Exams tab helps you track upcoming tests and board exams with visual countdowns. Keeping exam dates visible transforms distant deadlines into present motivations.

Adding an Exam

  1. Go to the ⏰ Exams tab
  2. Enter the Exam Name (e.g., "Unit Test 2 – Mathematics")
  3. Select the Exam Date using the date picker
  4. Choose the associated Subject from your subjects list
  5. Set Importance: 🔴 High (board exams), 🟡 Medium (unit tests), 🟢 Low (class tests)
  6. Click "➕ Add Exam"

Reading the Countdown Cards

Each exam shows a countdown card with:

  • 🟢 Green countdown: More than 7 days remaining — comfortable preparation time
  • 🟡 Yellow countdown: 1–7 days remaining — increase study intensity immediately
  • 🔴 Red countdown: 0 days or past — exam today or already over
⚠️ Important: When an exam shows less than 7 days, the Dashboard will automatically generate a red reminder notification. Use this as a signal to review your timetable and add extra revision sessions for that subject.

Section 6

Setting & Tracking Goals

Setting Your Weekly Study Goal

  1. Go to the 🎯 Goals tab
  2. Enter your target total study hours for the week (e.g., 14 hours = 2 hours/day)
  3. Click "💾 Save Goal"
  4. The goal progress bar will immediately reflect your current progress toward this target
💡 NEP 2020 Goal-Setting Tip: Start with a realistic goal based on your current study habits and increase by 10-15% each week. Studies show that challenging but achievable goals produce the greatest motivation and learning gains.

Goal Progress Bar

The large progress bar shows the percentage of your weekly goal completed:

  • 🟢 Green (100%+): Goal achieved — celebrate and set a higher goal next week!
  • 🟡 Yellow (60–99%): On track — keep your current pace
  • 🔴 Red (0–59%): Behind target — review your schedule and recommit

Weekly Summary

The summary automatically identifies:

  • Total hours studied this week
  • Most studied subject — your current strength
  • Neglected subject — what needs immediate attention next week

Section 7

Study Notes System

The Notes tab helps you capture key learnings immediately after each study session. This activates the generation effect — writing about what you learned significantly improves long-term retention, as supported by cognitive science research.

Adding a Study Note

  1. Go to the 📝 Notes tab
  2. Select the Subject this note relates to
  3. Enter a Topic (optional but recommended — e.g., "Chapter 5: Gravitation")
  4. Write your note in the text area: key concepts, formulas, things to review, questions for the teacher
  5. Click "💾 Save Note"
✅ NCF 2023 Metacognition Tip: After every study session, write at least 3 things you learned and 1 thing you're still unsure about. This brief reflection doubles your retention of the material studied.

Using Notes Effectively

Use notes to record: key formulas, chapter summaries, vocabulary, questions to ask your teacher, tricky concepts, connections between topics. Review your notes before each new session on the same topic.

Best Practices & NEP 2020 Tips

Weekly Routine (Recommended by NCF 2023)

  1. Sunday (Planning Day): Generate or review your timetable for the coming week. Set or adjust your weekly study goal. Check upcoming exam countdowns.
  2. Monday–Friday (Execution): Check Dashboard each morning. Follow the timetable. Start the timer for each session. Write a note after each session.
  3. Saturday (Review Day): Review the Weekly Summary in Goals tab. Identify the most and least studied subjects. Adjust next week's plan accordingly.

Subject Balance Strategy

The most common mistake is studying only favorite or easy subjects. NCF 2023 explicitly advocates holistic curriculum engagement. Use the Dashboard's weekly progress bars to ensure no subject bar is empty by Friday.

Timer Best Practices

  1. Study in 45-minute blocks followed by a 10-minute break (Pomodoro technique)
  2. Always select the correct subject before starting — your analytics depend on accuracy
  3. Don't stop the timer for 5-minute distractions — only stop for genuine breaks
  4. Aim for at least one session per subject per week to earn the "All-Rounder" badge
🌟 PARAKH Assessment Preparation: Competency-based assessments test deep understanding, not memory. Use the Notes tab to write explanations of concepts in your own words — this is the most effective preparation for competency-based testing.

Technical Guide

File Structure

The app consists of these files — keep them all in the same folder:

📁 study-planner-app/
  📄 index.html    ← Main application
  📄 concept.html  ← Conceptual background page
  📄 manual.html   ← This manual
  📄 manifest.json  ← PWA configuration
  📄 service-worker.js ← Offline caching
  🖼️ author.jpg    ← Author photo

Running the App

  1. Direct browser: Double-click index.html to open in Chrome (some PWA features require a server)
  2. VS Code Live Server: Install Live Server extension → Right-click index.html → "Open with Live Server"
  3. Python server: Open terminal in folder → type python -m http.server 8000 → open localhost:8000

Free Hosting Options

  1. GitHub Pages: Create a GitHub repository → Upload all files → Enable GitHub Pages in Settings → Your app is live at username.github.io/repo-name
  2. Netlify: Go to netlify.com → Drag your project folder into the deploy area → Instant live URL
  3. Cloudflare Pages: Connect GitHub repository → Cloudflare auto-deploys on every update

Converting to Android APK

  1. Host the app online (use GitHub Pages or Netlify from above)
  2. Go to pwabuilder.com
  3. Enter your app's URL and click "Start"
  4. PWABuilder will analyze your app and generate an Android package
  5. Download the APK file and install it on Android (enable "Unknown Sources" in settings)
  6. For Play Store submission: use the AAB package from PWABuilder and upload to Google Play Console
📱 Play Store Note: A Google Play Developer account requires a one-time 5 registration fee. Once registered, you can publish unlimited apps and updates for free.
