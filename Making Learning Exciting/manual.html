1. Getting Started

LearnSpark requires no registration, no password, and no internet after the first load. Simply open the app in any modern browser on a phone, tablet, or computer.

  1. Open the App
    Open index.html in Chrome, Firefox, or Safari. If hosted online, navigate to the URL provided by your institution or teacher.
  2. Install for Offline Use (Recommended)
    Look for the "Install App" banner at the bottom of the screen, or use your browser's "Add to Home Screen" option. After installation, the app works without internet.
  3. Explore the Navigation
    Use the tab bar at the top (desktop) or bottom (mobile) to move between sections. Your progress saves automatically to your device.
  4. Start with Curiosity Lab
    For first-time users, the Curiosity Lab is the ideal starting point — it demonstrates the app's spirit of discovery without requiring any prior knowledge.
💡 Tip for Teachers: Introduce the app during a 15-minute class session. Ask students to open the Curiosity Lab and pick one question to explore — then briefly discuss their findings as a class warm-up.

2. Home / Landing Page

🏠
Home Tab
Your starting point and overview dashboard
  • Purpose: The landing page orients users to the app's purpose and displays live progress statistics.
  • Stats Row: Shows your total challenges completed, curiosity topics explored, pathways enrolled, and badges earned. These update automatically as you use the app.
  • CTA Buttons: "Start a Challenge" and "Explore Curiosity Lab" take you directly to those sections.
  • Policy Badges: The NEP 2020, NCF 2023, PARAKH, and NCERT tags remind users of the app's policy grounding.
  • Author Card: Displays Mateen Yousuf's identity and role as concept developer.

3. Real-Life Connections Tab

🌍
Real-Life Connections
Making every subject feel relevant and meaningful
  • Purpose: Shows students how school subjects appear in real-world contexts they recognise. Directly implements NCF 2023's "meaningful learning" principle.
  • 8 Subject Cards: Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Geography, History, Language, Economics — each with a real-world connection story.
  • How to use: Students read each card and tap to mark it explored. Progress is tracked toward the Connections Discovered statistic.
  • Classroom Use: Teachers can assign 2–3 cards per week and ask students: "Where did you see this in real life since last class?"
  • Discussion Starter: Each card ends with an implicit question — use these as 5-minute discussion prompts to open lessons.
Teaching Note: These cards are conversation starters, not complete explanations. Encourage students to extend the connection: "Can you think of another example of Chemistry in your daily food?"

4. Learning Challenges Tab

⚡
Learning Challenges
Project-based missions that develop PARAKH competencies
  • 4 Challenges: Plastic-Free School (Environmental Science + Maths), Family Budget Planner (Maths + Life Skills), Climate Scientist (Science + Geography), Tech Inventor (Science + Creative Thinking).
  • Progress Steps: Each challenge has 5 steps. Tap "Mark Step Complete" after finishing each step. The progress bar fills as you advance.
  • Completing a Challenge: All 5 steps must be marked. At completion, you earn progress toward the "Problem Solver" and "Learning Champion" badges.
  • Individual or Group: Challenges can be done solo or in groups of 2–4 students. Group presentations are especially effective for classroom learning.
  • No Right Answer: Each challenge is open-ended — students design their own solutions. Evaluate based on reasoning quality and application of concepts, not a single correct answer.

How to Run a Challenge in Class

  1. Introduce the Challenge (5 min)
    Read the challenge description aloud with the class. Discuss: "Have you seen this problem in real life? Why does it matter?"
  2. Group Formation (3 min)
    Form groups of 3–4 students. Each group works on the same challenge or different challenges depending on your objective.
  3. In-App + In-Notebook (20 min)
    Students use the step guide in the app while recording their actual findings and designs in their notebooks or a shared document.
  4. Presentations (10–15 min)
    Groups present their solutions. Classmates ask questions. This develops communication skills alongside subject knowledge — aligned with NCF 2023.
  5. Mark Progress & Reflect (2 min)
    Students mark completed steps in the app. Brief whole-class reflection: "What was the most surprising thing you discovered?"

5. Curiosity Exploration Lab

🔭
Curiosity Lab
Eight "big questions" with genuinely surprising scientific answers
  • 8 Questions: Airplanes, smartphones, seasons, ocean salt, memory, volcanoes, medicine, and the universe — each revealing deep scientific principles through everyday wonder.
  • Tap to Reveal: Tap any card to reveal the answer. Tap again to close. Explored topics are counted toward your Curiosity Explorer badge and stats.
  • Classroom Ritual: Use as a weekly "Curiosity Minute" — one question per week, briefly discussed at the start of class. This builds the habit of scientific questioning.
  • Curiosity Wall: Teachers can write each week's question on the board and invite students to guess before revealing the app's explanation. This activates prior knowledge.
  • Cross-Subject Connections: Each answer deliberately connects multiple subjects — e.g., how memory works connects biology, psychology, and practical study advice.
💡 Best Practice: After revealing an answer, ask students: "What new question does this answer create for you?" Post their new questions on a "Curiosity Wall" in the classroom. These student-generated questions are often more motivating than teacher-assigned topics.

6. Learning Pathways Tab

🗺️
Interest-Based Learning Pathways
Personalised learning directions aligned with NEP 2020
  • 4 Pathways: Technology & Innovation, Environment & Sustainability, Business & Economics, Creative Arts & Communication.
  • Enroll: Students click "Enroll in Pathway" on any pathway they are interested in. Enrollment is tracked in Progress and the Teacher Dashboard. Students can enroll in multiple pathways.
  • Subject Connections: Each pathway shows which school subjects it draws from — helping students see the purpose of their curriculum in relation to their interests.
  • Not a Separate Syllabus: Pathways do not add new content — they reorganise existing subjects around a meaningful direction, as recommended by NCF 2023.
  • Career Awareness: Use pathways to introduce the concept that different careers draw on different combinations of school subjects — building early career awareness.

7. My Progress / Tracker Tab

🏆
Achievement Tracker
Your personal learning journey record
  • 4 Statistics: Challenges Completed, Curiosity Topics Explored, Pathways Enrolled, Connections Discovered — all auto-calculated from your activity.
  • Badges: Four earnable badges: Curiosity Explorer (3+ curiosity topics), Problem Solver (2+ challenges), Learning Champion (all 4 challenges), Pathfinder (2+ pathways). Earned badges glow gold; locked badges appear greyed out.
  • Learning Notes: A private notebook for reflections, questions, or ideas. Type anything and click "Save Notes" — saved automatically to your device. No one else can see these notes.
  • Persistence: All data is stored in your device's browser storage. It survives app restarts and offline sessions. Only a browser cache clear will reset progress.
Note: Progress is stored per device per browser. If a student switches devices or browsers, their progress will not transfer. For shared devices, students should export their notes manually.

8. Teacher Engagement Dashboard

📊
Teacher Dashboard
Classroom activity overview and pedagogical resources
  • Usage Statistics: Shows cumulative app engagement statistics (challenges, curiosity explorations, pathway enrollments) from this device session.
  • Suggested Activities: Five ready-to-use classroom activities that extend the app's philosophy into face-to-face teaching, each with a time estimate.
  • Policy Alignment Guide: Four cards explaining how the app aligns to NEP 2020, NCF 2023, PARAKH, and NCERT — useful for teacher reports, inspections, or explaining the app to administration.
  • Teacher as Facilitator: The dashboard reinforces NEP 2020's vision of the teacher as a learning facilitator rather than a content deliverer. Use it to spark discussion, not as a monitoring tool.

Suggested Weekly Schedule

  1. Monday: Real-Life Connection (5 min)
    Begin the week's first lesson by asking students which Real-Life Connection card relates to this week's topic. Brief discussion before teaching.
  2. Wednesday: Curiosity Minute (3 min)
    Mid-week, reveal one curiosity question from the Curiosity Lab. Students guess the answer before it's revealed. Post follow-up questions on the Curiosity Wall.
  3. Friday: Challenge Progress (10–15 min)
    Groups report their challenge progress. Celebrate completed steps. Discuss what students found surprising or difficult.
  4. Monthly: Pathway Showcase (15 min)
    Students who enrolled in pathways briefly share one interesting thing they discovered about their pathway area. Peer inspiration and cross-pathway awareness.

9. Micro-Learning Tab

⚡
Micro-Learning Experiences
Short, focused articles on learning psychology and education philosophy
  • 4 Articles: Why Curiosity Makes You Smarter; The Power of Connecting Knowledge; Growth Mindset: The Science of Effort; Real Learning vs Exam Learning.
  • Length: Each article is 3–4 minutes reading time — short enough to maintain attention, long enough to develop a complete idea.
  • Target Audience: These are written for students, not teachers. The language is direct and engaging, addressing students about their own learning.
  • Classroom Reading: Teachers can assign one article per week as a reading activity and facilitate a 5-minute discussion about its message.
  • Highlighted Insights: Each article contains a highlighted "key insight" box — these are ideal as discussion starters or wall posters for classroom display.

10. Installing the App

LearnSpark is a Progressive Web App — it can be installed on any device like a native app, without requiring the Google Play Store or App Store.

📱
Android Phone
  1. Open the app in Chrome browser
  2. Look for the "Install App" banner at the bottom of the screen
  3. Tap "Install App" and confirm
  4. The app will appear on your home screen like any other app
  5. It works offline after installation
💻
Desktop / Laptop
  1. Open the app in Chrome
  2. Look for the install icon (⊕) in the address bar
  3. Click it and select "Install"
  4. The app opens in its own window like a desktop application
  5. Find it in your Start Menu or Applications folder
🍎
iPhone / iPad
  1. Open the app in Safari browser
  2. Tap the Share button (□↑) at the bottom
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Tap "Add" to confirm
  5. The app icon appears on your home screen
🏫
School Lab / Shared Computer
  1. Open the app in any browser
  2. No installation needed for shared computers
  3. Students can use it directly in the browser
  4. Progress saves per browser — one profile per student ideal
  5. Students can bookmark the URL for quick access

11. Offline Usage

After the first visit and installation, LearnSpark works completely without internet. The service worker caches all app files — HTML, CSS, JavaScript — to your device's storage.

💡 For Rural Areas: Open the app once on a WiFi or data connection to allow caching. After that, all 8 tabs, all 4 challenges, all curiosity questions, and all micro-learning articles are available offline, indefinitely.

All student progress (challenge steps, curiosity explorations, pathway enrollments, notes) is stored in LocalStorage — the browser's built-in local database. This data persists between sessions without any internet connection.

12. Frequently Asked Questions

Will my progress be lost if I close the app? +
No. All progress is automatically saved to your device's browser storage as you interact with the app. Closing and reopening the browser or app will retain all your data. The only way to lose progress is to clear your browser's cache/cookies, or use a different device.
Does the app need internet to work? +
Only for the very first load and to download the author photo. After that, the app is fully cached and works completely offline. This is by design — to ensure access in areas with limited connectivity across India.
Can teachers see each student's individual progress? +
In the current version, the Teacher Dashboard shows aggregate statistics from the current device session, not individual student tracking. For class-wide data, teachers can ask students to verbally report their progress, or use the challenge presentation format where groups share their work directly.
How do challenges relate to the actual school curriculum? +
All four challenges are designed around concepts covered in NCERT textbooks for Classes 8–10. The Plastic-Free School challenge applies environmental science and basic mathematics. The Budget Planner uses arithmetic and consumer mathematics. The Climate Scientist applies physics, chemistry, and geography. The Tech Inventor integrates science with creative design thinking. Teachers can assign challenges after teaching the relevant chapters.
Is this app aligned with NEP 2020 and NCF 2023? +
Yes — this is the foundational design principle. Every module was created in direct reference to specific policy requirements: joyful learning (NEP 2020), meaningful and experience-connected learning (NCF 2023), competency-based assessment (PARAKH), and curiosity-driven pedagogy (NCERT). See the Conceptual Background document for complete policy mapping.
What subjects and grade levels is this suitable for? +
LearnSpark is designed for Classes 7–10 as the primary audience, but can be used effectively from Class 6 through Class 12. The app is subject-agnostic — it develops motivation and engagement as meta-skills that improve performance across all subjects. The Real-Life Connections module specifically covers mathematics, science (all branches), geography, history, language, and economics.
How long should students spend on the app each session? +
The app is designed for flexible use. A focused 5-minute curiosity exploration fits within a lesson. A full challenge takes 30–60 minutes and can be spread across multiple sessions. The micro-learning articles are 3–4 minutes each. The app is most effective when integrated into regular class routines rather than used as a one-time activity.
